TIM'S STORY

Hello !
My name is Tim Smith. I am an IT administrator and this is my story.

I monitor all the data centres through single sign-on application called Unity Dashboard. It gives me health status and related reports of all servers, networks and storage systems.

For last three months, few server reports are not in good health and need continuous attention and remediation. This has made my life difficult as I need to run reports everytime I reach office.

I need a way to autorun reports and get it delivered directly to my team’s mailbox daily, weekly or monthly so that I can take necessary actions in advance.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

How Might We design an intuitive way to run the reports automatically at required intervals like daily, weekly or monthly.
The report outcomes should be emailed to the team.
Make the whole process of scheduling simple, lightweight and consistent.

PROBLEM SPACE

RESEARCH

PRIMARY RESEARCH

Multiple interactions with stakeholders and end users revealed useful insights about the context and the need of the scheduling autorun of reports.

Few insights were -

  • Need of scheduled reports to ease the monitoring task as it keeps users from manually running when it’s critical.

  • Need of notification on the next run/next update so that users don’t miss on the critical updates.

SECONDARY RESEARCH

Secondary research on implementation of schedulars in different scenarios was done and the study on the patterns available uncovered the findings mentioned below.

SECONDARY RESEARCH FINDINGS

1. One schedule per instance works best.
2. There are four fields to set the frequency -frequency type, specific frequencies, start time and end time.
3. Frequency selection options are hierarchically dependent.
4. . There could be almost unlimited combinations of scheduling frequencies are possible, so instead of trying to cater for all needs, it should focus on the primary scenario.

IDEATION

GENERATING IDEAS

Out of six different solution approaches, two ideas primarily came out.
1. Schedule of a report as an independent entity with all CRUD operations
2. Multiple schedules attached to a report with CRUD operations and a lifecycle.
3. Single schedule for each report with On/Off states.
4. Progressive disclosure for all scheduling options.

USABILITY TESTING

PLANNING THE TEST

Aimed at uncovering the usability issues and validation of few key design decisions, the usability test was planned with 5 app concepts to be validated, corresponding tasks and few follow-up questions.

A quick usability tesing was carried out to validate the solution and few key design decisions.

Valuable Insights

The custom options for daily and weekly removed considering as edge cases.

Auto selected current timestamp/days are easier and gives cues of valid inputs.

The idea of having only one schedule at a time works well for simplicity.

A/B Testing to validate the placement of icons and other options in the interface using Hubspot.com.

ICON CONCEPT

ICON & THE CONTEXT OF USAGE

Designed a new icon for report sceduling option with consistent visual language of Grommet design system.

3 States of the Icon

Icon in grey shows that there are no saved schedules for the report, however it can be scheduled.

Icon in green shows that there is a saved schedule for the reports.

No Icon represents that the reports do not have an option to be scheduled.
