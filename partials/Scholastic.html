Designing e-commerce platform

Scholastic is the world's largest publishers, distributors of children's books and a leader in educational technology. In addition to some most popular book series like Harry Potter Series and The Hunger Games, the company is well known for its school book clubs, book fairs, classroom magazines and educational technology programs.

PROJECT GOALS

THE BACKGROUND

This one-year assignment was an initiative to ramp up the online sales and conversion. It also focussed on engaging customers and guiding them to buy products and subscribe to the services.
As an individual contributor I also led the team of three with one front-end developer and two graphic designers.

PROBLEM STATEMENT & CHALLENGES

Transforming a content intensive web portal into an ecommerce application for consumers, book-distributors and wholesale retailers.
The challenges are as follows -

DESIGN PROCESS

The project started with primary and exhaustive secondary research. All the information of this content intensive site was restructured. Then, the iterative prototyping was done followed by testing at the end of each iteration.



  1. User and Market Research
  2. Competition Analysis
  3. Information Architecture
  4. Wireframes & Prototypes
  5. Usability Testing

RESEARCH

Limited feasibility to user research, the secondary research was reliable on the analytics data. The data analytics was designed in order to understand the users and derive meaningful insights out of it.

COMPETITION ANALYSIS

The online presence and performance of competitors were evaluated on different usabiity, content and overall experience parameters. The data was consolidated then to extract the positive and negative experiences.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

The information peices were first grouped at high level, then chunked in the defined groups and the taxonomy was created. Hybrid card sorting was used to define the high level of groups using optimalworkshop studies.

WIREFRAMES & PROTOTYPES

Interactive prototypes were created and tested. The iterative design appraoch refined the wireframes and improved the usability and experience.
